How to play Mascarade

Mascarade is a very simple game to pick up and play, with only a couple options each turn, but every game feels completely different. Unlike most traditional card games, you aren't allowed to look at your card, except under certain circumstances. Turns progress like most games, with each person getting their turn in order in a circle, until the conditions for a game to end are met

On your turn you may do one of four actions. Note that when a game starts, the only two actions allowed for the first 4 turns are Swap and Fake. Once a player reaches 13 gold, or any player drops to 0 gold, the player with the most gold wins the game (there can be ties!).

Actions

Switch your card with another player

Fake a switch with another player. To all other players (including your target) this will look idential to a regular swap

Look at your own card. Other players will know you've done this, and it uses up your turn. Just don't be surprised if someone swaps you right afterwards!

Claim a character's ability. When you do this, every other player is given the opportunity to contest your claim. If anyone contests, you and all of the players who contested must reveal your cards. Whoever has the card that was claimed gets to do that action, even if it wasn't their turn! Also note that if nobody successfully reveals the card that was claimed, nobody gets to take that action. If anyone contests, everyone who reveals their card but does not win the contest must pay 1 gold to the courthouse. Here are the different effects of the characters in the game (note that a specific subset of cards is used depending on how many players are in the game)

Characters

The Judge takes all gold from the courthouse.

The Bishop takes 2 gold from the richest player. If the player using it is the richest, they take from the second richest.

The King gains 3 gold.

The Fool gains 1 gold, then picks two other players in the game. The fool may choose to swap their cards, or fake a swap.

The Queen gains 2 gold.

The Theif takes 1 gold from each player adjacent to them.

The Witch chooses a player and swaps all gold with them.

The Spy looks at their own card, picks another player and looks at that player's card, and then can either swap or fake a swap with that player.

The Peasant gains 1 gold. If there are 2 peasants both revealed in the same turn, each of them gain 2 gold instead of 1.

The Cheat wins the game if activated when the player has 10 or more gold.

The Inquisitor picks a player and interrogates them. That player must guess their own card. If they're right, nothing happens. If they're wrong, the Inquisitor takes 4 gold from them.

The Widow gains enough gold to put her at exactly 10.
